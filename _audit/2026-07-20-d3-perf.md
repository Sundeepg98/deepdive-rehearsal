## 1. Instrument (how the numbers were taken)

- Repo Chromium (playwright 1.61), `file://` the built 11.7MB deliverable, launched with the gate's
  own `_boot.cjs` (`launchOpts`/`gotoApp`/`enterApp`/`settle`) so boot/readiness match the house.
- CPU throttle via CDP `Emulation.setCPUThrottlingRate` (4x), **verified each cycle by a calibration
  spin** (a fixed busy loop, JIT-warmed): its 4x/1x ratio must be ~4, and its absolute 4x time is the
  per-cycle **LOAD covariate** — an inflated spin means a loaded box.
- **First-visit semantics:** each pane's first activation is measured in its OWN fresh reload (the
  target is the 2nd pane of the session, after walk), so no warm layout leaks between samples.
- **Metric:** in-page wall-clock. `t0` just before a synthetic click through the real
  `onclick→goView→Router.navigate→emit→switchTab` path (all synchronous — verified in `router.js`
  `emit()` and `view-transitions.js` `run()` which no longer defers), then rAF to first painted
  frame. `sync` = the click task (JS + any forced layout); `toPaint` = input→first paint (what the
  user feels).
- **Attribution:** the Chromium timeline trace (`browser.startTracing`) decomposed by self-time into
  script / style-recalc / layout / paint. **Never the longtask API** (it drops big throttled tasks at
  4x — this track's documented measurement trap).
- Scripts live untracked in `_perf/` (`attrib.cjs`, `trace.cjs`, `ab.cjs`, `ceiling.cjs`); raw JSON in
  `_perf/results/`. The base build is snapshotted at `_perf/builds/base.html` (sha256 verified ==
  HEAD's committed deliverable).

**Load honesty (up front):** the box is shared by four builders and was **loaded for these runs**
(calibration ratio ~5.8 vs the ~4.0 of a calm box; 4x spin band 95–369ms vs the ~158ms calm point).
Per this track's law and its three errata, the **absolute milliseconds below are load-inflated**; the
**decomposition and rankings are load-invariant** and are the durable content. Deltas are reported as
matched-load paired A/B (the track's own resolution: "matched-load ratios govern").

## 2. ATTRIBUTION — the audit measured the symptom, mis-attributed the mechanism

### Item #2 — drill first-activation (the P1). Audit hypothesis: *"prewarm probe 1 so activation is a display toggle"* (i.e. `drawCard()`/`_mustHit()` script is the cost).

**REFUTED. The cost is reveal LAYOUT, not script.** Trace self-time of a single drill first-activation
(@4x, 3 reps, under load):

| term | ms (self, p50) | what it is |
|---|---|---|
| **Layout** | **~217** | laying out the drill shadow subtree the instant `.pane.on` toggles `display:none→block` |
| UpdateLayoutTree (style recalc) | ~57 | style resolution over the revealed subtree |
| EventDispatch (self) | ~38 | the click dispatch machinery |
| Paint / PrePaint | ~26 | |
| **FunctionCall (all JS, incl. `drawCard`+`_mustHit`)** | **~3.8** | the script the audit blamed |

Mechanically why: `drawCard(0)` already runs **eagerly at topic-load** (`DeepDrill` is `eagerTopic`;
`renderTopic → setMode('study') → renderD → drawCard(0)` builds the first card into the still-hidden
shadow root), and `_mustHit` only runs at the final reveal stage (not stage 0). So the card DOM
already exists before first activation; the activation pays the **reveal layout** of a previously
`display:none` subtree. `sync ≈ toPaint` (99%) confirms it's forced synchronously inside the click
task, not deferred to the render phase. **Prewarming `drawCard` would save ~4ms of a ~275ms cost** —
and would needlessly reach into `drawCard`'s region (owned by the product wave) for no gain.

### Item #8 — five panes first-visit (trade/model/wb/num/sys). Audit hypothesis: *"idle-prebuild the next-likely pane."*

**INFEASIBLE as stated. Same cost (reveal layout), and you cannot prebuild it.** Every pane's
first-activation `sync ≈ toPaint`, i.e. the cost is the same forced reveal layout of its shadow
subtree. The RENDER is already deferred-then-done by `TopicPaneQueue` (the panes' innerHTML exists in
their shadow roots by the time they're first shown); what's expensive is the **LAYOUT**, and a
`display:none` subtree **cannot be laid out ahead of time** — layout is skipped precisely because it's
`display:none`. "Idle prebuild" addresses a render cost that is already paid; it cannot touch the
layout cost that remains.

### Item #9 — topic-open variance (180–332ms). Audit hypothesis: *"defer/chunk the bank reseed off the critical path."*

**REFUTED. The cost is the room-retint style recalc + walk relayout, not the reseed.** Trace
self-time of a topic switch (@4x, 3 reps, under load):

| term | ms (self, p50) | what it is |
|---|---|---|
| **UpdateLayoutTree (style recalc)** | **~146** | `applyIdentity` sets `data-group` on `<html>` → the six-rooms accent re-tint → full-document style recalc (every `var(--acc)` consumer) |
| **Layout** | **~110** | the visible walk pane + identity header relayout |
| Paint / ParseHTML | ~42 | |
| **FunctionCall (incl. `publishBanks` — the "reseed")** | **~5.7** | the script the audit blamed |

`publishBanks` (the bank reseed) is **5.7ms of array `.slice()`/`.map()`**. The real cost is the
**room re-tint style recalc**, which is inherent to the *shipped, test-guarded* six-rooms accent
system (`data-group` → every accent token retints; do NOT rebuild — see the project's accent-rooms
note). The **variance** the audit flagged is substantially **box-load contention**, not a fixed
deferrable cost: on a calm box the topic-open tightens.

### One-line summary
**All three audit perf mechanisms are inverted or infeasible.** The costs are `display:none`→visible
**reveal layout** (panes) and the six-rooms **retint style recalc** (topic-open) of a very large
single-file document — not the script-level operations (`drawCard`, prebuild, reseed) the audit named.
This is the "CONFIRMED mechanically, INVERTED financially" pattern the perf track already has on
record, one level deeper.

## 3. Mechanism — `content-visibility:auto` on the two flagged card-list breaches; evidenced no-op on the rest

The only attribution-supported lever for the reveal-layout term is `content-visibility:auto` on
below-fold pane cards (candidate #3 from the 2026-07-18 attribution): it lets the browser skip the
layout of cards below the fold on the first reveal, laying out only what's on screen. VR is captured
at the **viewport** (1280×800), so below-fold cards it skips are already outside the capture — the
at-rest pixels are unchanged.

### The CEILING test (runtime-injected, zero build) — which panes the lever actually reaches

Injecting `content-visibility:auto` into a pane's shadow root right before its first activation, paired
against the un-injected reveal on the **same** build under the **same** load (matched-load paired A/B,
the delta cancels box load). `d` = injected − baseline (negative = faster); MATCHED = cycles whose
base/branch load spins agree within 15%:

| pane (selector) | matched d (ms) | n | verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| **trade** (`#tdecs > .dec`) | **−250 to −325** | 3 | huge win — a clean vertical card stack |
| **rf** (`#rflist > *`) | **−137** | 3 | big win — but rf is NOT a breach (~80ms calm, the audit's fast half) |
| **num** (`#out > *`) | **−28** | 8 | modest win — a flagged breach (150ms) |
| **sys** (`#chain > *`) | +8 | 6 | NO win — content fits within ~1.5 viewports |
| **wb** (`#list > *`) | +168 (n=1, noisy) | 1 | NO win |
| **drill** (`.dnav-wrap` block) | **−12** | 6 | NO reach — the drill P1's cost is ABOVE-fold |
| **drill** (`#dnav .dn-step` grid items) | −11 | 6 | NO reach (grid items don't skip) |

**The drill P1 is not addressable by this lever.** Its ~250ms reveal is above-the-fold shadow-subtree
layout+recalc (scoreboard, mode/tier toggles, first probe card) — content-visibility can only skip the
`.dnav` below the fold, which is ~11–12ms of the cost. Fixing the P1 would mean restructuring the
drill's visible content (out of scope — the product wave owns drawCard's region) or a broader shadow
change; neither is justified for a first-visit-only, load-inflated, one-time-per-topic cost.

### CALM-WINDOW BATTERY (executed 2026-07-20 on the merged tree, box verified quiet) — the lever was MEASURED, then RETRACTED

The ceiling proves the lever's *potential*; the ship proof is a real base-build vs branch-build on a
calm box. That proof was run at the merge window (master `794fe8a`, D4+D5+D2 merged; box quiescence
measured — 0 ms-playwright, all agents parked). Two things it found, in order:

**(a) A latent boot bug in my own edit (fixed, `f871cfc`).** The `.dec` comment inside the
`TRADE_STYLE` *backtick* template literal wrapped `auto` in backticks for emphasis — which terminates
the string, leaving a stray `auto` identifier: `Unexpected identifier 'auto'` at parse time, halting
app init before `window.switchTab`/`TopicRegistry` wired (60s `APP_READY` hang). Latent since
`53c418f` because pre-merge I only ever runtime-INJECTED content-visibility (ceiling) and DEFERRED the
real-build A/B — a build of the source edit was **never boot-verified until the calm window**. The
lesson is the wave's: the ceiling's runtime injection and the deferred A/B between them left a build
path untested; boot-verify a real build the moment you edit source, don't lean on injection.

**(b) The A/B win is real and large — but the lever is NOT VR-clean, so it is DROPPED (`24c030f`).**

Real-build matched-load paired A/B, @4x, calm box (matched = base/branch load-spins within 15%):

| pane | base p50 | branch p50 | **matched dPaint** | matched n | note |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **trade** `.dec` | 160.1 | 48.9 | **−105.3 ms** | 7/8 | heaviest first-visit pane — a big real win |
| **num** `.nrow` | 135.7 | 87.9 | **−30.4 ms** | 4/8 | clears the −15ms keep-bar; confirms ceiling's −28 |
| drill (control, untouched) | 259.2 | 239.5 | −8.3 (noise) | 6/8 | no regression |
| sys (control, untouched) | 132.4 | 132.3 | −0.9 (flat) | 5/8 | no regression |

*(Deflation note: the calm trade win −105ms is well below the loaded ceiling's −250..−325 — the ceiling
base was load-inflated (217ms) where the calm base is 160ms, so the absolute saving is smaller. num
−30.4 ≈ ceiling −28. The rankings held; the absolutes deflated, exactly as this track predicts.)*

**Then the VR proof — and it FAILS.** `content-visibility:auto` implies `contain: layout style paint`
on the element, applied to ON-screen cards too, not only the below-fold skip. That perturbs sub-pixel
text rasterisation across the whole *visible* pane (base-build vs branch-build, 1280×800, gate's
CHANNEL_TOL=2):

| pane | px changed (light) | px changed (dark) | worst Δ | where |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **trade** | **16 828** | 16 519 | 212/255 | content area, bbox 366,189→879,767 — **not** the scrollbar |
| **num** (guarded `num-light`) | **13 022** | 13 000 | 213/255 | content area |

Screenshots base-vs-branch are **visually identical** to the eye — the deltas are ~1px glyph-edge
flips (a sub-perceptual shift), and the pane's total scroll-height also moves (3250→3156). But the VR
gate budget is **`MAX_CHANGED = 32` px/baseline**, and `visual_regression.cjs` documents that budget as
deliberately sized to catch *exactly* this: "a sub-perceptual colour drift over a large area" still
fails (the budget is on the COUNT, not the magnitude). num is ~400× over budget → the **guarded gate
rejects it**; trade fails its zero-VR proof (`_perf/vrcheck.cjs`) the same way.

**Decision — DROP both (forced by the untouchable ZERO-VR invariant, not a fork).** The deliverable is
reverted to byte-identical with master (`sha256 3e143e6…`). A fold-tuned `nth-child(n+K)` variant that
would leave the at-rest viewport uncontained *could* pass the single baseline — but it games the one
captured topic's fold position, is fragile across the 46 topics' varying card heights, and is exactly
the brittle-guard pattern this repo warns against. Rejected on principle.

**What the wave ships, then:** the attribution instrument (`_perf/*.cjs`, for the cold verifier + all
future perf work) and this report — and an **honest negative result**: these first-visit costs are
**Layout-bound**, and the only attribution-supported lever that reduces Layout (containment) **cannot
meet the pixel-lock**. Under the locked visual identity, items #2/#8/#9 are **WON'T-FIX by a CSS lever**
— a valid, evidenced no-op, which the brief explicitly blesses. The value delivered is the killed
hypotheses (§2: prewarm/prebuild/reseed all inverted) + this proven ceiling, so no future wave re-chases
any of them, content-visibility included.

**NOT pursued (evidenced):** rf (a real ceiling win but not a breach — gilding a non-problem), sys/wb/
model (no lever), the **drill P1** (no reach — cost is above-fold), **topic-open** (inherent six-rooms
retint recalc — item #9 no-op).
