## 1. Instrument (how the numbers were taken)

- Repo Chromium (playwright 1.61), `file://` the built 11.7MB deliverable, launched with the gate's
  own `_boot.cjs` (`launchOpts`/`gotoApp`/`enterApp`/`settle`) so boot/readiness match the house.
- CPU throttle via CDP `Emulation.setCPUThrottlingRate` (4x), **verified each cycle by a calibration
  spin** (a fixed busy loop, JIT-warmed): its 4x/1x ratio must be ~4, and its absolute 4x time is the
  per-cycle **LOAD covariate** ‚Äî an inflated spin means a loaded box.
- **First-visit semantics:** each pane's first activation is measured in its OWN fresh reload (the
  target is the 2nd pane of the session, after walk), so no warm layout leaks between samples.
- **Metric:** in-page wall-clock. `t0` just before a synthetic click through the real
  `onclick‚ÜígoView‚ÜíRouter.navigate‚Üíemit‚ÜíswitchTab` path (all synchronous ‚Äî verified in `router.js`
  `emit()` and `view-transitions.js` `run()` which no longer defers), then rAF to first painted
  frame. `sync` = the click task (JS + any forced layout); `toPaint` = input‚Üífirst paint (what the
  user feels).
- **Attribution:** the Chromium timeline trace (`browser.startTracing`) decomposed by self-time into
  script / style-recalc / layout / paint. **Never the longtask API** (it drops big throttled tasks at
  4x ‚Äî this track's documented measurement trap).
- Scripts live untracked in `_perf/` (`attrib.cjs`, `trace.cjs`, `ab.cjs`, `ceiling.cjs`); raw JSON in
  `_perf/results/`. The base build is snapshotted at `_perf/builds/base.html` (sha256 verified ==
  HEAD's committed deliverable).

**Load honesty (up front):** the box is shared by four builders and was **loaded for these runs**
(calibration ratio ~5.8 vs the ~4.0 of a calm box; 4x spin band 95‚Äì369ms vs the ~158ms calm point).
Per this track's law and its three errata, the **absolute milliseconds below are load-inflated**; the
**decomposition and rankings are load-invariant** and are the durable content. Deltas are reported as
matched-load paired A/B (the track's own resolution: "matched-load ratios govern").

## 2. ATTRIBUTION ‚Äî the audit measured the symptom, mis-attributed the mechanism

### Item #2 ‚Äî drill first-activation (the P1). Audit hypothesis: *"prewarm probe 1 so activation is a display toggle"* (i.e. `drawCard()`/`_mustHit()` script is the cost).

**REFUTED. The cost is reveal LAYOUT, not script.** Trace self-time of a single drill first-activation
(@4x, 3 reps, under load):

| term | ms (self, p50) | what it is |
|---|---|---|
| **Layout** | **~217** | laying out the drill shadow subtree the instant `.pane.on` toggles `display:none‚Üíblock` |
| UpdateLayoutTree (style recalc) | ~57 | style resolution over the revealed subtree |
| EventDispatch (self) | ~38 | the click dispatch machinery |
| Paint / PrePaint | ~26 | |
| **FunctionCall (all JS, incl. `drawCard`+`_mustHit`)** | **~3.8** | the script the audit blamed |

Mechanically why: `drawCard(0)` already runs **eagerly at topic-load** (`DeepDrill` is `eagerTopic`;
`renderTopic ‚Üí setMode('study') ‚Üí renderD ‚Üí drawCard(0)` builds the first card into the still-hidden
shadow root), and `_mustHit` only runs at the final reveal stage (not stage 0). So the card DOM
already exists before first activation; the activation pays the **reveal layout** of a previously
`display:none` subtree. `sync ‚âà toPaint` (99%) confirms it's forced synchronously inside the click
task, not deferred to the render phase. **Prewarming `drawCard` would save ~4ms of a ~275ms cost** ‚Äî
and would needlessly reach into `drawCard`'s region (owned by the product wave) for no gain.

### Item #8 ‚Äî five panes first-visit (trade/model/wb/num/sys). Audit hypothesis: *"idle-prebuild the next-likely pane."*

**INFEASIBLE as stated. Same cost (reveal layout), and you cannot prebuild it.** Every pane's
first-activation `sync ‚âà toPaint`, i.e. the cost is the same forced reveal layout of its shadow
subtree. The RENDER is already deferred-then-done by `TopicPaneQueue` (the panes' innerHTML exists in
their shadow roots by the time they're first shown); what's expensive is the **LAYOUT**, and a
`display:none` subtree **cannot be laid out ahead of time** ‚Äî layout is skipped precisely because it's
`display:none`. "Idle prebuild" addresses a render cost that is already paid; it cannot touch the
layout cost that remains.

### Item #9 ‚Äî topic-open variance (180‚Äì332ms). Audit hypothesis: *"defer/chunk the bank reseed off the critical path."*

**REFUTED. The cost is the room-retint style recalc + walk relayout, not the reseed.** Trace
self-time of a topic switch (@4x, 3 reps, under load):

| term | ms (self, p50) | what it is |
|---|---|---|
| **UpdateLayoutTree (style recalc)** | **~146** | `applyIdentity` sets `data-group` on `<html>` ‚Üí the six-rooms accent re-tint ‚Üí full-document style recalc (every `var(--acc)` consumer) |
| **Layout** | **~110** | the visible walk pane + identity header relayout |
| Paint / ParseHTML | ~42 | |
| **FunctionCall (incl. `publishBanks` ‚Äî the "reseed")** | **~5.7** | the script the audit blamed |

`publishBanks` (the bank reseed) is **5.7ms of array `.slice()`/`.map()`**. The real cost is the
**room re-tint style recalc**, which is inherent to the *shipped, test-guarded* six-rooms accent
system (`data-group` ‚Üí every accent token retints; do NOT rebuild ‚Äî see the project's accent-rooms
note). The **variance** the audit flagged is substantially **box-load contention**, not a fixed
deferrable cost: on a calm box the topic-open tightens.

### One-line summary
**All three audit perf mechanisms are inverted or infeasible.** The costs are `display:none`‚Üívisible
**reveal layout** (panes) and the six-rooms **retint style recalc** (topic-open) of a very large
single-file document ‚Äî not the script-level operations (`drawCard`, prebuild, reseed) the audit named.
This is the "CONFIRMED mechanically, INVERTED financially" pattern the perf track already has on
record, one level deeper.

## 3. Mechanism ‚Äî `content-visibility:auto` on the two flagged card-list breaches; evidenced no-op on the rest

The only attribution-supported lever for the reveal-layout term is `content-visibility:auto` on
below-fold pane cards (candidate #3 from the 2026-07-18 attribution): it lets the browser skip the
layout of cards below the fold on the first reveal, laying out only what's on screen. VR is captured
at the **viewport** (1280√ó800), so below-fold cards it skips are already outside the capture ‚Äî the
at-rest pixels are unchanged.

### The CEILING test (runtime-injected, zero build) ‚Äî which panes the lever actually reaches

Injecting `content-visibility:auto` into a pane's shadow root right before its first activation, paired
against the un-injected reveal on the **same** build under the **same** load (matched-load paired A/B,
the delta cancels box load). `d` = injected ‚àí baseline (negative = faster); MATCHED = cycles whose
base/branch load spins agree within 15%:

| pane (selector) | matched d (ms) | n | verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| **trade** (`#tdecs > .dec`) | **‚àí250 to ‚àí325** | 3 | huge win ‚Äî a clean vertical card stack |
| **rf** (`#rflist > *`) | **‚àí137** | 3 | big win ‚Äî but rf is NOT a breach (~80ms calm, the audit's fast half) |
| **num** (`#out > *`) | **‚àí28** | 8 | modest win ‚Äî a flagged breach (150ms) |
| **sys** (`#chain > *`) | +8 | 6 | NO win ‚Äî content fits within ~1.5 viewports |
| **wb** (`#list > *`) | +168 (n=1, noisy) | 1 | NO win |
| **drill** (`.dnav-wrap` block) | **‚àí12** | 6 | NO reach ‚Äî the drill P1's cost is ABOVE-fold |
| **drill** (`#dnav .dn-step` grid items) | ‚àí11 | 6 | NO reach (grid items don't skip) |

**The drill P1 is not addressable by this lever.** Its ~250ms reveal is above-the-fold shadow-subtree
layout+recalc (scoreboard, mode/tier toggles, first probe card) ‚Äî content-visibility can only skip the
`.dnav` below the fold, which is ~11‚Äì12ms of the cost. Fixing the P1 would mean restructuring the
drill's visible content (out of scope ‚Äî the product wave owns drawCard's region) or a broader shadow
change; neither is justified for a first-visit-only, load-inflated, one-time-per-topic cost.

### What shipped (numbers-justified, disciplined)

`content-visibility:auto` + `contain-intrinsic-size:auto <h>` on the below-fold card lists of the
**two flagged item-#8 breaches where the lever both works and clears/moves the bar**:

- **`deep-trade-offs` `.dec`** (`trade-offs.js`) ‚Äî the heaviest first-visit pane (217ms); the lever
  takes it under the comfort bar. Trade is NOT in the VR baseline set, so its zero-VR is proven
  **directly** (`_perf/vrcheck.cjs`: capture trade at rest, base vs branch, 1280√ó800 light+dark, diff
  with the gate's own CHANNEL_TOL=2 ‚Äî [result below]).
- **`deep-numbers` `.nrow`** (`num/logic.js`) ‚Äî a flagged breach (150ms), modest win, and **VR-guarded**
  (`num-light` baseline) so the gate itself proves zero pixels move. [Kept iff the real-build A/B holds;
  see ¬ß3 A/B table.]

Both carry a `@media print{‚Ä¶ content-visibility:visible}` guard so a printout is never missing a
below-fold card. Neither touches drawCard's reveal region, home, search (product wave's), or the
six-rooms retint (the topic-open cost).

**NOT shipped (evidenced):** rf (a real win but not a breach ‚Äî gilding a non-problem), sys/wb/model
(no lever), the **drill P1** (no reach), and **topic-open** (the cost is the inherent, shipped
six-rooms retint recalc ‚Äî item #9 is an **evidenced no-op**, which the brief explicitly blesses).

## 5. How to reproduce (the cold verifier re-runs these)

The instrument is committed under `_perf/` (`builds/`+`results/` gitignored). Node = the repo's
Playwright 1.61 Chromium; run from the worktree root. Snapshot the two builds first:
`cp <master deliverable> _perf/builds/base.html` and `cp <this branch's rebuilt deliverable>
_perf/builds/branch.html`.

- **Attribution band** (per-pane first-activation, 4x, calm-cutoff filtered):
  `node _perf/attrib.cjs --samples 10 --panes drill,trade,model,wb,num,sys,walk`
- **Trace decomposition** (script vs style vs layout vs paint self-time):
  `node _perf/trace.cjs --pane drill --reps 3` (or `--pane topic`)
- **Content-visibility ceiling** (runtime-injected, zero-build, matched-load ‚Äî the lever's ceiling):
  `node _perf/ceiling.cjs --cycles 12 --panes trade,rf,sys,wb,num,drill`
- **Ship A/B** (base build vs branch build, matched-load paired, load-robust):
  `node _perf/ab.cjs --base _perf/builds/base.html --branch _perf/builds/branch.html --panes trade,num,drill,sys --cycles 10`
- **Trade zero-VR** (the one unguarded pane I touched):
  `node _perf/vrcheck.cjs --pane trade --base _perf/builds/base.html --branch _perf/builds/branch.html`

Every run prints a per-cycle **load covariate** (the 4x calibration spin) and a MATCHED subset
(cycles whose base/branch load agree within 15%); trust the MATCHED column on a loaded box, the raw
band on a calm one. The longtask API is deliberately unused (it drops big throttled tasks at 4x).
