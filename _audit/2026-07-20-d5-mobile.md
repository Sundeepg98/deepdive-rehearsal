   aria-keyshortcuts="N">` inside `.mockcta`, between the mock CTA and the Tools FAB. Focus order:
   Mock run -> NextUp chip -> Tools.
2. **`src/styles.css`** — (a) base `.nd-m{display:none}` (hides it on desktop and by default);
   (b) in the `@media(max-width:919px)` bottom-bar block, `.nd-m:not([hidden])` becomes a compact
   quiet pill (neutral `--card` bg, hairline `--bd` border, accent kicker + arrow), `order:1` (Tools

3. **`src/scripts/app/session-progress.js`** — `flowDock()` refactored into `flowDockDesktop()`

   is a no-op) + `flowDockMobile()`, both fed by ONE `nextUp()` result. The mobile chip: shows the

both fire only on meso/macro.

## Anti-goal 8 ("absorb, never stack"), addressed explicitly (ratified by team-lead)

Anti-goal 8's "absorb, never stack" targeted a **second bottom surface competing with the bar** — a
NextUp dock rendered *beside* or *below* the mock/tools bar, forcing a second row (the exact failure
the inttog's `flex-basis:100%` caused: a 123px two-row bar clipping the drill question). This chip
creates **no second surface**: one bar, the Tools FAB unchanged, zero stacking — the chip is a third
child of the *existing* single flex row. So the anti-goal's target condition (a competing second
surface / a second row) **never arises**. The chip is conformant to anti-goal 8 by construction, not
an unbuilt "absorb." (Reading ratified by team-lead, 2026-07-20.) The one-row invariant is *enforced*
in the gate: `mobile_nextup` asserts the bar height is identical chip-shown vs chip-hidden, and the
nowrap plant proves that assertion can go red.

## The one iteration (measured, not guessed)

First prototype at 360px grew the bar to **91px** (a regression). Root cause, measured:
`mockLblLines: 2` — the 120px mock button (squeezed by the chip) wrapped "Mock run" to two lines,
growing the mock button to 66px and the fixed bar to 91px. This is exactly the class of drift the
`position:fixed` pin exists to prevent (a bar that resizes across the micro<->meso boundary). Fix:
`white-space:nowrap` on the mobile mock label — the bar height is now **independent of the chip
width**. Re-measured: bar 72px at both 360 and 390, mock label one line, no clip.
